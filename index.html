SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

What is SDG 11?

Sustainable Development Goal 11 aims to "Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable." It recognizes that urbanization presents both challenges and opportunities for sustainable development.

Key Targets of SDG 11:

  • Safe and affordable housing: Ensure access for all by 2030
  • Sustainable transport: Provide accessible and safe transportation systems.
  • Inclusive urbanization: Enhance sustainable and inclusive urban planning.
  • Cultural heritage: Protect and safeguard the world's cultural and natural heritage.
  • Disaster reduction: Reduce deaths and economic losses from disasters.
  • Environmental impact: Reduce the adverse per capita environmental impact of cities.
  • Green spaces: Provide universal access to safe, inclusive green and public spaces.

Why SDG 11 Matters?

More than half of the world's population lives in cities, and this number is expected to grow to 68% by 2050. Cities occupy just 3% of Earth's land but account for 60-80% of energy consumption and 75% of carbon emissions. Making cities sustainable is crucial for our planet's future.
